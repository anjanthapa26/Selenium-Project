Position Summary:
We are looking for a DevOps Engineer who understands and embraces a DevSecOps philosophy and has successful experience supporting a DevSecOps Team. You will work closely with development, operations, and security to design and implement solutions, as well as support/ensure access to automation tools for our government client. Specifically, you will be responsible for managing CI/CD pipelines and cloud application environments that support rapid and reliable configuration management, as well as continuous integration, build, release, and monitoring solutions. Lastly, you will be responsible for ensuring that all supporting systems are operational and maintain various levels of certification.

Responsibilities:
Effectively prioritize work and complete tasks on time.
Able to work and collaborate with a remote team.
Assist with software and infrastructure management, inventory, installations, configurations, and connectivity.
Provide support for administering web and application servers.
Evaluate and implement technologies that improve efficiency, performance, and reliability.
Analyze requirements and recommend best fit architecture.
Build solutions that are generic, scalable, and reusable.
Collaborate with other engineers to develop deployment and maintenance strategies for existing and new services.
Analyze and troubleshoot problems within DevSecOps tools.
Assist with various compliance activities, including vulnerability management and mitigation.
Maintain documentation related to processes, procedures, solutions, and architecture.
Required Qualifications:
Continuous integration and delivery (CI/CD).
Linux system administration experience.
Strong scripting/development skills.
Python and UNIX shell scripting are preferred.
Experience with test automation tools.
Experience with logging configuration and analysis.
Experience with Configuration Management tools such as Ansible, Chef, Puppet, and/or SaltStack
Preferred Qualifications:
Experience managing RHEL (Red Hat Enterprise Linux)
Familiarity with Artifactory.
Splunk experience is a plus.
Experience managing and configuring JBOSS, OpenShift, or Tomcat web containers
Experience with JBoss Fuse, Appian, and Oracle DB
Experience with GitLab, JIRA, and other Atlassian tools
Experience with IDEs, such as IntelliJ, Eclipse, or similar
Experience with Security/Compliance
Knowledge of DevSecOps processes, procedures, and tools (such as git, CI/CD, Configuration Management)
Expertise in cloud computing platforms such as AWS, Azure and Google Cloud
Expertise in Kubernetes
Expertise in building Docker images
Strong experience in deploying and managing microservices
Pipelines such as AWS CodePipeline and Jenkins

LCAT Job Requirements:
Ability to attain Federal Government Position of Trust.
Must be a U.S. citizen.
Must successfully pass a Federal background investigation.
